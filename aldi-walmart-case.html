ALDI VS. WALMART

An analysis of how a German hard-discounter disrupted the world's largest retailer, and how omnichannel fulfillment is reshaping the grocery landscape.

THE COLLISION COURSE

In 2019, Aldi asserted itself as a genuine challenger to US grocers, doubling its store count and encroaching on Walmart's territory by opening locations near Walmart Supercenters. While Walmart historically relied on its massive Vendor-Managed Inventory (VMI) logistics network and "Everyday Low Prices," Aldi utilized a hyper-efficient private-label model with only 1,400 curated items per store.

THE ALDI MODEL

  • Hard Discounter: Prices perceived to be 25% lower than conventional retailers.
  • Operational Efficiency: Cross-trained staff, display on pallets, custom barcodes for lightning-fast checkout.
  • Upscale Expansion: Moved from "food deserts" to upscale suburbs, introducing Specially Selected gourmet brands.
  • Aggressive Growth: Target of 2,500 stores by 2022, opening 130 stores per year.

THE WALMART PIVOT

  • VMI Mastery: Integrated ordering with vendors like P&G, reducing inventory costs by keeping products moving.
  • Format Struggle: Launched Neighborhood Markets to fight Aldi, but closed 130 underperforming locations.
  • Omnichannel Shift: Halted physical store expansion to focus on Click-and-Collect and same-day delivery.
  • New Frontiers: Diversifying profit sources through advertising and health care.
