Structural analysis and material optimisation for a mid-rise concrete frame. Applied FEA and fatigue testing to right-size load-bearing specifications without compromising safety margins.

Problem

The initial structural design for a reinforced concrete frame specified conservative material grades across all load-bearing members -- adding 12–18% to material cost with no engineering justification beyond precedent. The client needed a specification review backed by test data.

Action

Designed and executed a compressive strength and flexural fatigue test program across M25 and M30 concrete mixes under cyclic loading. Applied finite element analysis to model stress distribution, identify critical failure zones, and validate revised cross-section specifications against IS 456 code requirements.

Result

Test data supported a targeted downgrade of non-critical members to M25 -- reducing estimated material cost by 9% while maintaining full safety factor compliance. The methodology was adopted as the project's standard specification review protocol.

%

The % of demand cycles you want to fulfil without a stockout. 95% = 1 stockout in 20 order cycles. 99% costs significantly more to maintain.
